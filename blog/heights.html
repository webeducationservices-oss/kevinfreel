Stunning Newer 3 Bedroom Bungalow-Style Home with Open House This Saturday

Watch the video →

Finding a home that feels fresh, functional, and full of character is not always easy. Many buyers end up choosing between old-home charm and newer construction convenience. That is why this beautiful 3 bedroom, 2.5 bath attached home stands out in such a big way. Built in 2022, it brings together the timeless appeal of a 1920s bungalow with the comfort, style, and efficiency today’s buyers want most. From the inviting front porch to the detached 2 car garage, this is the kind of property that feels special the moment you see it. If you have been waiting for a peaceful, move-in-ready home with warmth and personality, this may be the one.

Newer 3 Bedroom Home with 1920s Bungalow Charm

There is something special about a home that feels classic without feeling dated. This property captures that balance beautifully. Although it was built in 2022, it offers the design details people love about historic bungalows. You get charm, texture, and character, but without the maintenance concerns that often come with an older home.

The exterior immediately sets the tone. The front porch welcomes you in with the kind of curb appeal that makes a lasting impression. There is a relaxed, neighborly feel here that fits perfectly with the timeless bungalow style. At the same time, the detached 2 car garage adds flexibility and function that modern homeowners appreciate.

This is the best of both worlds. You get the visual charm of a classic home and the confidence that comes with newer construction. For buyers who want beauty without compromise, that is a rare opportunity.

Covered Front Porch, Rear Porch, and Bright Bungalow Interior

One of the first things buyers notice about this home is how inviting the outdoor spaces feel. Both the front and rear porches have that charming metal roof look that helps give the property its bungalow personality. They are not just decorative either. These spaces truly add to the way the home lives.

The front porch is ideal for relaxing at the end of the day, greeting neighbors, or simply enjoying the setting. It creates a warm first impression and extends the living space outdoors in a very natural way. The rear porch offers a more private place to unwind, making it perfect for quiet mornings or peaceful evenings.

Step inside, and the home continues to impress. The interior carries that same 1920s-inspired feel with thoughtful details that make the space feel rich and welcoming. Engineered wood flooring adds warmth and continuity throughout the main living areas. Built-ins bring style and function. High ceilings make the home feel open and airy.

Then there is the natural light. With tons of windows throughout, the home feels bright, cheerful, and connected to the outdoors. That natural light gives every room an uplifting energy and helps highlight the craftsmanship and finishes throughout the home.

For buyers who love homes with personality, this interior strikes a great balance. It feels polished, but not stiff. It feels timeless, but still current. Most importantly, it feels like home.

Modern Kitchen with Center Island, Pendant Lights, and Stainless Steel Appliances

The kitchen is one of the standout spaces in this home, especially for buyers who want a layout that works for everyday life and entertaining. It offers a clean, beautiful design while still feeling comfortable and practical.

At the center of the kitchen is a spacious island that gives you room to prep, serve, gather, or simply sit and enjoy a cup of coffee. Whether you are hosting friends, helping with homework, or putting together a quick dinner after work, the island becomes a natural hub of activity.

Pendant lighting adds style and warmth above, helping define the space and enhance the home’s overall design character. The pantry gives you valuable storage, which is always a plus, and the stainless steel appliances bring a sleek, modern finish that buyers expect in a newer home.

This is the kind of kitchen that looks great in photos, but even better in person. It is functional, bright, and thoughtfully laid out. In today’s market, kitchens matter, and this one checks the right boxes.

Upstairs Primary Suite with Walk-In Closets and Frameless Glass Shower

Upstairs, the home continues to deliver the comfort and layout today’s buyers are looking for. The primary suite is a true retreat. It offers the kind of privacy and space that helps a home feel relaxing at the end of a busy day.

The bedroom itself is oversized, giving you more than enough room for a full furniture set while still leaving space to breathe. It feels open, calm, and removed from the main living areas in the best way. Generous walk-in closets provide storage that many buyers are hoping for but do not always find.

The primary bath adds another layer of appeal. Dual vanities make mornings easier, especially for couples, and the sleek frameless glass walk-in shower gives the space a polished, modern look. It feels clean, stylish, and easy to enjoy.

Two additional bedrooms upstairs offer versatility for the way people live today. They can be used for guests, children, hobbies, or a home office setup. That kind of flexibility is important, especially for buyers who need their home to adapt over time.

The home’s 2.5 bath layout is practical and convenient, giving both residents and guests the space they need. Another major plus is the upstairs laundry. Having the laundry area close to the bedrooms makes daily life easier and more efficient. It is one of those details that buyers truly appreciate once they experience it.

Fenced Outdoor Living Area with Turf, Gardens, and Irrigation

Outdoor space can make a big difference in how a home feels, and this property offers an outdoor setting that is both attractive and easy to enjoy. The rear porch is the perfect place to start your morning with coffee, unwind with a book, or simply take in the peaceful landscaping.

The fenced side and back area creates privacy while giving you a beautiful outdoor extension of the home. Turf helps keep the space looking neat and low maintenance, which is a major benefit for busy homeowners. Gardens add color, texture, and a sense of care that makes the property feel loved and well designed.

An irrigation system is another practical feature that supports the landscaping and helps maintain the home’s curb appeal over time. These details matter because they contribute to a lifestyle that feels easier, calmer, and more enjoyable.

This is not just a house with a backyard. It is a home with outdoor spaces that encourage you to slow down and enjoy where you live. That peaceful, relaxing feeling is a big part of what makes this property so appealing.

Why This Modern Bungalow Is Worth Seeing in Person

Online photos can spark interest, but some homes need to be experienced in person to be fully appreciated. This is one of them. The charm of the porches, the brightness of the interior, the warmth of the finishes, and the peaceful feel of the outdoor spaces all come together in a way that is hard to capture in just a few pictures.

For buyers looking for a newer home that does not feel generic, this property offers something different. It has character. It has style. It has comfort. It also has the practical layout and updated features buyers want right now.

From the detached 2 car garage to the upstairs laundry, from the center-island kitchen to the private primary suite, this home was clearly designed for modern living. Yet it still carries the charm and atmosphere of a classic bungalow. That combination is what makes it memorable.
